The Controversy

I don't pretend to know everything, I just only speak on matters I know I'll win

A competition where the wittiest tongues compete against each other on the trending issues and battle out their opponents with the power of their words.

Format

The Controversy will be a 2 phase competition event comprising Eliminations and Finals. The first round (Eliminations) will be a group-discussion wherein a variety of topics will be given to different lots. Each participant will be given specified time to present his views over the fiery issue. A pre-decided number of participants will qualify to the last round i.e-The Finale. In the last round; the finalists will debate on a current social, political or ethical issue to fire out the rest of them with the power of their words! Exciting hampers, cash prizes and much more await the winners.

Judging Criteria

  • Vocabulary, Fluency and Proficiency of the language
  • Presentation of views and content of speech
  • Their knowledge of the topic
  • Acquaintance with the norms of a standard debate

Rules

  • Participants need to register individually for the competition.
  • English will be used for all communication purposes.
  • The participant will not be allowed to carry any printed material.
  • Exceeding the time limit will impose penalty.
  • Order of speaking will be decided by conventional random measures.
  • Any correspondence with the judge should be through an OC (Organising Comittee) member.
  • The participant should refrain from using abusive content and give an equal opportunity to fellow speakers.
  • The OC reserves the right to change or modify any of the rules.

Spin the yarn

An ideal speaker doesnt require a base to set up a plot. He goes with the flow and yields out an amazing piece of art.

If you think that impromptu thoughts can transform a mundane story into an exciting yet fun-filled one, there exists no better alternative for you! An event based on the concept of plot building wherein a group of participants develop a story, initiating with a simple base line.

Format

Spin the Yarn will be a 2 phase competition event comprising Eliminations and Finals. Pre-decided number of participants will qualify for the second round based on their skills. In this competition, a base story line will be given by the moderator/jury member to begin the plot. The members of the team will thereafter continue the plot by adding a line, in accordance with the existing plot setup. The rules remain same for eliminations and the finale. Exciting hampers, cash prizes and much more await the winners.

Judging Criteria

  • Co-ordination between the team members.
  • The logic, quality and coherency of the resulting plot.
  • Proper usage of target language.
  • Distinction and relevancy of thoughts with respect to the story-line.

Rules

  • Spin the Yarn will be a group competition.
  • Minimum members=3 and Maximum members=4
  • Participant can use either of the languages- Hindi or English or a combination of both.
  • Every participant needs to speak at-least twice.
  • The length and elaboration of plot should be duly followed.
  • Any correspondence with the judge should be through an OC member.
  • The participants should refrain from using abusive content.
  • The OC reserves the right to change or modify any of the rules.

Poutpourri

Playing is the highest form of research

A fun-filled event to give everyone a break from lectures, projects and professors who sing lullabies.

Format

It is a three phase event. Participation will be in teams of 2. The first round is the game taboo. A card will be given to the participant with a main word and 5 others related to it. One person picks up the card and tries to explain that word to the other person without using the 5 other words. The second round is pictionary. A card is given to one member and he/she has to explain to the team mate what the card says by drawing. Sheet and pencil will be provided. The third round is dumb charades. A word/phrase will be given to one member and he/she has to explain it to the other team member by using actions only.

Judging Criteria

  • Speed. Which team guesses the maximum number of things in the given time.
  • Abiding by the rules.
  • Coordination between the members.

Rules and regulation

  • For taboo, no actions are allowed and the words in the card must not be spoken.
  • For Pictionary, only the medium of drawing must be used. No words or actions are permitted.
  • For Dumb Charades, the word/phrase must be explained by actions only.
  • All the other rules will be disclosed on the spot by the quiz master.
  • All decisions made by the Judges are final.
